TEACHINGS
Lineages & Teachers

At the heart of the Sufi tradition is a principle both ancient and practical: that any teaching genuinely useful and beneficial for human growth, learning, understanding, and being is worthy of sincere study and respect. Not as comparison, not as collection — but as living practice. The paths gathered here reflect that principle. Sufi heart-opening, Fourth Way self-observation, Kundalini technology, Siddha transmission, Zen directness, Tibetan precision, Ashtanga integration, plant medicine encounter, somatic release, and perspectives from outside ordinary frameworks — each tradition offers something specific that the others cannot fully replace. Together they form a single ongoing inquiry into what it means to become more awake, more whole, and more of use in the world.

MURSHID ISA LOVE DRAGON
SEPTEMBER 23, 1943  ·  DECEMBER 21, 2025

Isa was my teacher, my initiator into the Sufi path, and for twenty years my closest friend — the kind of friend that is rare enough in a lifetime to make you understand what the word actually means. He saw people clearly and loved them anyway. His presence had a quality that is difficult to name and impossible to manufacture.

Without him, the Dream Factory Sound Sanctuary would not exist as it does. He empowered its founding and shaped its spirit. In a real sense, the Dream Factory continues to carry and transmit something of his state — an aspect of his life's work made tangible in instruments, in space, in the artists who pass through it.

May his memory be a blessing.

SUFI
Opening the heart until love dissolves the distance between self and all things — the path of union.
Hazrat Inayat Khan → Murshid Sam Lewis → Murshid Hassan → Murshid Isa Love Dragon → Murshida Rudi Kadre
FOURTH WAY
Waking up from the sleep of mechanical existence through rigorous self-observation in the midst of ordinary life.
G.I. Gurdjieff · A.R. Orage · Thomas de Hartmann · C.S. Nott · Rodney Collin · E.J. Gold
KUNDALINI YOGA
A precise technology of the nervous system — breath, movement, and sound to awaken latent energy and expand the capacity to hold consciousness.
Guru Nanak → Guru Ram Das → Yogi Bhajan → Isa Love Dragon
SIDDHA YOGA
The direct transmission of awakened energy from teacher to student — and the recognition of pure consciousness as the ground of all experience.
Bhagavan Nityananda → Swami Muktananda → Swami Rudrananda (Rudi)
ZEN / CH'AN
Pointing directly at the nature of mind — stripping away every concept until only immediate, wordless awareness remains.
Shakyamuni Buddha → Bodhidharma
TIBETAN BUDDHISM
A vast science of mind — mapping consciousness, death, and liberation with extraordinary precision and depth.
Chögyam Trungpa · Ayang Rinpoche · Namkai Norbu · Ganteng Tulku · Tenzin Wangyal Rinpoche
ASHTANGA YOGA
Breath, movement, and focused gaze unified into a single moving meditation — steadying the body to still the mind.
T. Krishnamacharya → Sri K. Pattabhi Jois → Tim Miller
MESTIZO / AMAZONIAN
Healing and vision through the intelligence of plant medicine — direct encounter with the living spirit of the natural world.
Don Jose Campos — Mestizo tradition · Camp Munay, Peru
SOMATIC PRACTICE
The body as teacher — releasing trauma held in the nervous system and recovering the felt sense of being fully alive.
Peter Levine — Somatic Experiencing · Feldenkrais Method · Anat Baniel Method
CONTEMPORARY & METAPHYSICAL
Perspectives from outside conventional frameworks that expand the map of what is possible and who we truly are.
Bashar (Darryl Anka) · Michel Desmarquet
